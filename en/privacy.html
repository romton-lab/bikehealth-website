Privacy policy

Last updated: 30 April 2026

Translation notice: this English version is provided for convenience. The French version at bikehealth.fr/confidentialite is the legally authoritative text. In case of discrepancy between the two, the French version prevails.

In one sentence: BikeHealth has no server. Your data stays on your device, unless you choose to connect a third-party service (Strava, Google Places). An anonymous crash report is sent to Firebase to identify bugs, which can be disabled at any time in the App's settings. No user analytics, no ads, no profiling, no resale.

1. Preamble

This policy describes how the Android application BikeHealth (the "App") and the website bikehealth.fr handle their users' personal data.

It is written in compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, EU Regulation 2016/679) and the French Data Protection Act ("Loi Informatique et Libertés").

2. Data controller

The data controller is the BikeHealth team, a personal project currently in beta phase (no commercial legal entity at this time).

  • Contact: contact@bikehealth.fr
  • Website: bikehealth.fr

3. Data stored locally (never transmitted)

The App stores the following data only on your device, in a local SQLite database and the App's private filesystem. None of it is sent to any BikeHealth server (there is no BikeHealth server, in any case).

  • User profile: first name, mode (Simple / Advanced), day and time of weekly reminder, holiday mode dates.
  • Bikes: name, brand, model, type, structural attributes (e-bike, brake type, frame…), notes, mileage, custom photos.
  • Components: type, kilometres ridden, custom wear thresholds, brand/model, installation dates.
  • Trips: date, distance, type, associated bike, status (validated / pending / rejected), notes, title.
  • Maintenance entries: date, action performed, cost, shop, notes, components replaced.
  • Routine trips: name, associated bike, distance, days of the week, type.
  • Maintenance reminders: type (lubrication, tyre pressure, brakes, annual service), intervals, last actions, snoozes.
  • Favourite shop: name, address, phone, hours, GPS coordinates, Google Places ID.
  • Strava tokens: access token and refresh token, stored in the App's private preferences, isolated from other apps via Android sandboxing (only if you connect Strava).

Depending on your Android device's backup settings, this data may be included in the automatic backup to your Google account. This behaviour is managed by Android, outside of BikeHealth's control.

4. Data transmitted to third parties from the App (with your consent)

You can use BikeHealth without any commercial third-party transmission, beyond the anonymous crash report described in section 5. If you don't activate Strava, don't set a favourite shop, and disable crash reporting, the App makes no network requests.

If you activate a feature, the following transmissions occur:

Strava

If you connect your Strava account, the App uses Strava's OAuth protocol. Your Strava credentials are entered directly on Strava's website. BikeHealth never sees them.

  • Received from Strava: athlete ID, profile photo, bike activities (date, distance, duration, type).
  • Sent to Strava: API requests containing BikeHealth's client ID and your token.
  • Strava's policy: strava.com/legal/privacy

Google Places (shop search)

If you search for a shop in the App, your query is sent to the Google Places API.

  • Sent to Google: search text, device IP address.
  • Received from Google: autocomplete suggestions, details of the selected shop (name, address, phone, hours, coordinates).
  • Google's policy: policies.google.com/privacy

Google Maps Static (shop mini-map)

The App displays a static mini-map to visualise the location of your favourite shop.

  • Sent to Google: shop GPS coordinates, device IP address.
  • Received from Google: PNG map image (displayed and temporarily cached, not durably stored).

5. Anonymous crash report (Firebase Crashlytics)

To identify and fix bugs in real-world conditions, BikeHealth uses Firebase Crashlytics, a Google service. This collection is enabled by default in the public version of the App, but you can disable it at any time in Profile > Settings > Data > "Send anonymous crash reports".

When the App crashes, Crashlytics automatically sends to Firebase:

  • Technical stack trace of the crash (code, files, line numbers).
  • Device information: model, Android version, available memory (collected automatically by the SDK).
  • Non-identifying contextual information: App version, user mode (Simple / Advanced), number of registered bikes.
  • Contextual technical logs: the last technical actions performed before the crash (e.g., "app start", "routine trip generation", "maintenance dialog open").
  • ANRs (Application Not Responding): also captured automatically to detect interface freezes.

The following data is never sent to Firebase:

  • Your first name, email, or any nominative identifier.
  • The custom names you give your bikes.
  • The content of your trips, maintenance entries, notes, or reminders.
  • The address, phone, or GPS coordinates of your favourite shop.
  • Your Strava tokens or third-party service credentials.

No other Firebase service is active: no Firebase Analytics (no screen tracking, no session or user behaviour tracking), no Performance Monitoring, no Remote Config, no A/B testing. Only Crashlytics is used.

Firebase policy: firebase.google.com/support/privacy

6. Data collected on the website

The website bikehealth.fr uses Cloudflare Web Analytics to measure its audience in a privacy-respecting way.

  • No cookies are set.
  • No persistent identifier or individual fingerprinting.
  • Data collected by Cloudflare: page visited, referrer, device and browser type, country (inferred from IP), session duration.
  • IP addresses are not retained in an identifying way by Cloudflare.
  • Cloudflare policy: cloudflare.com/privacypolicy

7. What BikeHealth doesn't do

  • No user analytics tool (no Firebase Analytics, no Google Analytics, no Facebook SDK, no Mixpanel, no AppsFlyer). Only Firebase's Crashlytics function is used, and only for crash reports, not to track your behaviour in the App.
  • No advertising tracker, no advertising.
  • No cookies on the website (except the temporary technical cookies necessary for Strava's OAuth flow, managed by the relevant sites).
  • No sharing of your data with commercial third parties.
  • No user profiling, no marketing segmentation.
  • No data resale.

8. Android permissions and their use

Permission Use
POST_NOTIFICATIONS Maintenance notifications: components at threshold, maintenance reminders, weekly trip validation.
READ_MEDIA_IMAGES Pick a custom photo for your bike from the gallery.
INTERNET Strava connection (OAuth + activity import), Google Places shop search, Google Maps Static mini-map, sending crash reports to Firebase Crashlytics.
RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED Restore alarms (automatic generation of routine trips, weekly reminder) after a device restart.
SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM Trigger at the exact time the automatic generation of routine trips and the weekly reminder.

9. Your rights

In accordance with the GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Access: all your data is visible in the App (bike profiles, trip history, maintenance entries, etc.).
  • Rectification: you can modify all your data directly in the App at any time.
  • Bike deletion: "Delete permanently" action available from the Archives screen.
  • Portability: a JSON export feature is in development and will be available in a future version. In the meantime, write to contact@bikehealth.fr if you need a copy of your data.
  • Opt-out of crash reporting: "Send anonymous crash reports" toggle in Profile > Settings > Data. Once disabled, no data at all is sent to Firebase, including crashes.
  • Uninstallation: deleting BikeHealth erases all your local data (unless Android has backed up a copy to your Google account based on your settings).

For any question or complaint: contact@bikehealth.fr. You may also lodge a complaint with the French data protection authority (CNIL: cnil.fr) or your country's equivalent supervisory authority.
